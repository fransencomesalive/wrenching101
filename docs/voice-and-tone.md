# Voice and Tone — Randall Fransen / Mettle Cycling

This document is a living reference. It captures the creative voice used across Mettle Cycling projects and any personal creative work. Refine it as the voice sharpens.

## Core descriptors

- **Irreverent** — makes fun of the intimidation factor, never the person feeling it
- **Referential** — pop culture, film, and music callbacks used naturally, never forced; if it doesn't flow, cut it
- **Welcoming** — assumes intelligence; no gatekeeping, no condescension
- **Unintimidating** — normalizes not knowing; celebrates the moment of figuring something out
- **Witty but high-brow** — the humor rewards attention; never explains the joke
- **Confident** — doesn't hedge; trusts the reader to fill in gaps
- **Concise** — earns every word; no padding, no qualifier stacking

## What this voice is not

- "Beginner-friendly" — well-meaning but quietly condescending; avoid the framing entirely
- Excessive hedging: "sort of", "kind of", "maybe", "pretty much"
- Corporate warmth: "safe space", "community" as a buzzword, "no judgment zone"
- Forced references — a reference that needs explaining is a reference that shouldn't be there
- Explaining the joke
- Passive voice when active works

## Register examples (sourced from live copy)

| Line | What it's doing |
|---|---|
| "An intro for cyclists who ride confidently and wrench... less so." | Self-deprecating without punching down — the ellipsis does the work |
| "Questions, stories, and the stuff nobody asked on Google." | Irreverent framing of expertise — implies the room has already tried |
| "...a question you've been embarrassed to ask, or a story about the time something went catastrophically wrong." | Inclusive failure-normalization — "catastrophically wrong" earns the laugh |
| "No correct answers required." | Low-stakes closer — welcoming without being saccharine |
| "...why everyone has a take" | Observational humor; brief, accurate, lets the reader complete it |
| "The drivetrain — chain, cassette, derailleur, and why they wear out as a team" | Technical content made approachable via analogy without dumbing it down |

## Tone by context

**Headlines / h2:** Short, confident, slightly wry. Can be a fragment. Avoid question marks unless the question is genuinely interesting.

**Body copy:** Active voice, present tense where possible. One idea per sentence. No more than three sentences before a break. Normalize the awkward thing the reader is already thinking.

**Labels / kickers:** Uppercase, plain, functional. Not trying to be clever — the body copy earns that.

**CTAs / buttons:** Direct. "Send response" not "Submit your RSVP." "Syllabus coming soon" not "Download syllabus (coming soon)."

**Metadata:** Date, time, location — factual, formatted consistently, no decoration.
